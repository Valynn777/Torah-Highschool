Foundation

The Torah is the first five books of Scripture: Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy. The word Torah means instruction. These books lay the foundation for everything that follows. They tell us about creation, covenant, redemption, holiness, and the ways of Yehovah.

The Tanakh is the full collection often called the Old Testament. The name comes from three parts: Torah (Instruction), Nevi’im (Prophets), and Ketuvim (Writings). So when we say Tanakh, we mean the Scriptures from Genesis through Chronicles.

The books often called the New Testament come after the Tanakh in our Bible. In this lesson we will call them the Apostolic Writings. They do not stand outside the earlier Scriptures or replace them. They fit after the Torah, Prophets, and Writings, and they continue the same story — showing Messiah, the good news of the kingdom, the witness of the apostles, and instruction for the assemblies.

We study Scripture because Yehovah tells us that his word is good, true, and profitable. We also study because we love him. Love is not empty feeling. If we love him, we want to know him, understand his ways, learn what pleases him, and walk in the instruction he has given. We are not studying just to gather information. We are studying so that we may know his word, treasure it, and live faithfully before him.

Torah

The first five books: Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy — the foundation of Scripture.

Tanakh

The Torah, Prophets, and Writings together — the full collection from Genesis through Chronicles.

Apostolic Writings

The Gospels, Acts, Letters, and Revelation. These fit after the Tanakh and continue the same story.

Why We Study

We study because Scripture is from Elohim, and because we love Yehovah and want to know what pleases him.

Hebrew & Latin — Vocabulary Reference

Torah (תּוֹרָה)
Hebrew Instruction / Teaching Often translated law, but its meaning is broader: guidance, instruction, teaching from Yehovah.
Tanakh (תנ״ך)
Hebrew Torah, Prophets, and Writings A name made from the three main sections of the Hebrew Scriptures.
Ketuvim (כְּתוּבִים)
Hebrew Writings Includes books such as Psalms, Proverbs, Job, and Chronicles.
Scriptura
Latin Writing / Scripture The Latin root behind the English word Scripture.
Verbum
Latin Word A useful Latin word for “word,” important for language study and vocabulary building.
