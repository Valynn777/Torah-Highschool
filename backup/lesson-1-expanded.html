Part 1: The Word in Action

Learn: Point & Purpose

Genesis 1–5 establishes who Elohim is and reveals the Torah as the framework of all reality. Because Yeshua is the Word made flesh, we see His nature in every creative act.

Order & Filling

Elohim first creates "spaces" (Light, Sky, Land) and then "fills" them. Torah is about bringing order to chaos.

The Presence

Mankind was made to be the Temple of Elohim—a place where the Creator and the created walk together.

✦ Commentary: The Meaning of the Fall (Gen 3)

The serpent’s goal was to make Adam and Chavah doubt the goodness of the Commandment. Sin is "turning away" from Life. The exile was the natural result of moving away from the Source of Life.

Bereshit (בְּרֵאשִׁית) — In the beginning. The very first word of Torah.
Bara (בָּרָא) — To create. Used only of Elohim's creative power.
Imago Dei — Latin for "Image of Elohim." Our source of dignity.

Part 3: History & Science

Scientific & Historical Context

The Law of "Kinds" (Biological Order)

Genesis 1 emphasizes life reproducing "after its kind." We see this as genetic boundaries. Elohim built stability into the world so it would be reliable.

Chronogenealogies (The Science of Time)

Genesis 5 records the decay of human longevity. It provides a physical record of the consequences of the Fall on the human body over centuries.
