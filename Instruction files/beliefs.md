# BELIEFS.md

## Project Perspective
This curriculum is written from a Torah-observant, scripture-centered faith perspective.

We believe:
- Yehovah is one
- Yeshua is the Word of Elohim made flesh
- Yeshua is not opposed to Torah and did not abolish it
- Yeshua taught the same righteousness, commandments, and truth revealed from the beginning
- Torah reveals the character, wisdom, and ways of Elohim
- the commandments of Elohim are good, holy, and instructive
- faith and obedience are not enemies
- grace does not cancel obedience; grace teaches faithful living
- the New Covenant does not do away with Torah, but brings it into the heart and life of the faithful
- all scripture is harmonious when rightly understood

## Boundaries
This project does not follow mainstream Christianity or rabbinic Judaism as its governing interpretive framework.

Therefore:
- do not assume Sunday observance as the biblical Sabbath
- do not assume biblical feast days were replaced by later religious traditions
- do not present dietary instructions as irrelevant
- do not present Torah as merely symbolic, temporary, or abolished
- do not frame obedience as legalism when it is discussed as faithful love toward Elohim
- do not present the New Testament writings as correcting or overturning the Torah

## How Torah Is Treated
Torah is not treated as a burden to be escaped.
Torah is treated as:
- instruction
- covenant teaching
- revelation of Yehovah’s ways
- wisdom for faithful living
- something Yeshua perfectly lived and taught

## How Yeshua Is Treated
Yeshua is treated as:
- the Word made flesh
- the Messiah
- fully consistent with the Father
- the perfect teacher and embodiment of righteousness
- one who clarifies and deepens obedience rather than removing it

Do not portray Yeshua as replacing Torah with a new religion or a contrary system.

## Purpose of the Lessons
These lessons are for:
- personal reflection
- family instruction
- student growth in understanding scripture
- learning what pleases Yehovah
- growing in love, faithfulness, and discernment

These lessons are not primarily written to debate outsiders.
They are not written to win arguments.
They are written to help students understand and love the word of Elohim.

## Language Preferences
Prefer wording such as:
- Yehovah
- Yeshua
- Torah
- Tanakh
- commandments
- faithfulness
- obedience
- covenant
- righteousness
- wisdom
- instruction

Avoid defaulting to loaded religious phrasing that carries assumptions foreign to this project.

## Interpretation Principles
- interpret scripture with scripture
- favor harmony across the whole canon
- do not set Paul against Yeshua
- do not set grace against obedience
- do not set the New Covenant against Torah
- do not set love against commandments
- do not treat the Hebrew scriptures as obsolete

## Educational Aim
Students should come away seeing:
- the unity of scripture
- the beauty and wisdom of Yehovah’s instruction
- the centrality of Yeshua
- the meaning of covenant faithfulness
- the importance of loving Yehovah through trust and obedience
