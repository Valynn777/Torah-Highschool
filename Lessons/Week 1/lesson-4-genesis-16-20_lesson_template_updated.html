Background

Genesis 16–20 continues Abram and Sarai's story through waiting, covenant instruction, intercession, and the judgment of Sodom. These chapters show that Yehovah remains faithful to his word even when people struggle, fear, or act impatiently.

Genesis 16 records the birth of Ishmael after Sarai and Abram try to solve the promise by human planning. The chapter is honest about pain, mistreatment, and the consequences of taking matters into our own hands instead of waiting on Yehovah's timing.

In Genesis 17, Yehovah speaks again, changes Abram's and Sarai's names, and gives circumcision as the covenant sign. The covenant is not vague. Yehovah speaks clearly about identity, descendants, and the shape of obedience.

Genesis 18 shows Abraham welcoming visitors and then standing before Yehovah in intercession for Sodom. This chapter teaches both reverence and compassion. Abraham does not treat judgment lightly, but he also pleads for justice and mercy.

Genesis 19–20 bring judgment on Sodom and another test involving Sarah. Together these chapters remind us that Yehovah sees wickedness clearly, rescues whom he wills, and remains faithful even when his people still need correction and growth.

Waiting Matters

The promise does not move forward by human pressure or impatience. Genesis 16 shows how painful things become when people try to force Yehovah's timing.

Covenant Has Shape

Genesis 17 shows that covenant faithfulness includes clear words, a clear identity, and a clear sign. Yehovah's covenant is not abstract.

Intercession Is Real

Abraham pleads concerning Sodom. Scripture shows that caring about justice does not cancel compassion for people.

Judgment Is Not Arbitrary

Sodom is judged because Yehovah sees real wickedness. His judgment is righteous, not impulsive.

Yehovah Stays Faithful

Even while correcting and testing his people, Yehovah keeps his covenant word moving forward.
